The Adventures of
Priscilla,
Queen of the Desert

Australia, 1994  ·  Directed by Stephan Elliott  ·  Rated R

Two drag queens and a trans woman pile into a beat-up lavender bus and drive across the Australian Outback to perform a cabaret show in Alice Springs. Equal parts hilarious, heartfelt, and fabulous, it's a cult classic that holds up as both comedy and road movie. Hugo Weaving, Guy Pearce, and Terence Stamp are unforgettable. Winner of the Academy Award for Best Costume Design.

Date Friday, March 20th Arrive 6:00 PM (movie starts at 7:00 PM) Location The Fox Den Runtime 1 hr 44 min Bring Drinks & snacks to share (comfy clothes welcome; fabulous clothes more welcome)
Content Warnings

This is a beloved film, but it was made in 1994 and has some content worth knowing about beforehand. We wanted to be upfront so everyone can decide how to engage with it.

  • Homophobic and transphobic slurs - including "f*ggot" and "tr*nny," used both in-universe as attacks and, in one case, casually between characters
  • Anti-LGBTQ+ violence - a drag queen is chased and attacked by a group of men in a threatening scene that includes an implied threat of sexual assault (intervened before it escalates)
  • Transphobia / family rejection - a character references her parents cutting off contact after her transition
  • Implied childhood sexual abuse - a brief flashback depicts an adult relative making inappropriate advances toward a child; the child escapes and the scene is treated as darkly comic
  • Outdated terminology - the word "transsexual" is used throughout, which is the language of the era
  • Sexist / racist stereotyping - a Filipino character is written as a broad stereotype, which critics have noted as a genuine flaw of the film
  • Heavy profanity - pretty much constant throughout
  • Crude sexual humor - including an extended bar scene with explicit (though mostly off-screen) sexual acts played for comedy
  • Alcohol - characters drink heavily; one scene involves a drinking contest to the point of passing out
✦ Fun Fact

The actor who plays the lead drag queen "Tick" is Hugo Weaving - yes, the same guy who later became Agent Smith in The Matrix and Lord Elrond in The Lord of the Rings. Priscilla was only his second major film role. His first was the 1991 Australian drama Proof - and then he walked straight into this. The Matrix was still five years away.

Despite all of the above, we love this movie and think you will too.
